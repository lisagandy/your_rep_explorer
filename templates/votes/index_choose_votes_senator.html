In the United States Congress, most senators vote with their party on all issues, therefore when a senator does vote against the majority of his/her party this occurrence is of interest. When a senator votes against his/her party they might be influenced by many causes, such as campaign contributions or their constituency which might be affected by the particular vote. Congressional Close Up tags votes where one or more senator goes against party and automatically finds possible influences on the senator.
